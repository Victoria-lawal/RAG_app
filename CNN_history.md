# Research: CNN history

### **Summary of CNN’s History**

#### **Founding and Launch (1980)**
- **Founder:** CNN was founded by **Ted Turner** and the **Turner Broadcasting System**.
- **Launch Date:** June 1, 1980.
- **Innovation:** It was the **first 24-hour cable news channel**, revolutionizing television news by providing continuous coverage.
- **Initial Broadcast:** The first telecast was anchored by **Dave Walker and Lois Hart**.

#### **Key Milestones and Achievements**
- **1980s:**
  - **Live Coverage of Major Events:** CNN was the **only network to broadcast live** during key moments, such as the **1981 assassination attempt on President Ronald Reagan**.
  - **First Live Presidential Interview:** In 1985, CNN conducted the **first live interview with a sitting U.S. president (Ronald Reagan)**.
  - **Larry King Live (1985–2010):** Became one of CNN’s most popular programs, featuring high-profile interviews.

- **1990s:**
  - **Persian Gulf War (1991):** CNN provided **live "in-country" coverage** from both sides of the conflict, with correspondents like **Bernard Shaw, Peter Arnett, and John Holliman** gaining global recognition.
  - **First Live Web Interview (1995):** CNN.com broadcast the **first live interview with a president (Bill Clinton)**.

- **2000s–Present:**
  - **21st-Century Anchors:** Notable figures include **Anderson Cooper, Don Lemon, Kaitlan Collins, and Jim Acosta**.
  - **Programs:** *Anderson Cooper 360°* (2003–present) and *The Situation Room* (2005–present) became staples.
  - **Live Coverage of Breaking News:** CNN was the **first to break major stories**, including the **9/11 attacks (2001)** and **COVID-19 pandemic (2020)**.
  - **Controversies & Challenges:** CNN faced criticism for perceived bias, legal disputes (e.g., **Trump-era clashes**), and competition from **Fox News and MSNBC**.

#### **Technological and Cultural Impact**
- **Satellite Technology:** CNN’s use of **satellite broadcasting** enabled **global live coverage**, setting a new standard for news dissemination.
- **Competition:** While initially dominant, CNN later faced competition from **Fox News (1996)** and **MSNBC**, leading to shifts in cable news dynamics.
- **Digital Expansion:** CNN expanded into **online news (CNN.com)** and **social media**, maintaining relevance in the digital age.

#### **Legacy**
- **Pioneer of 24/7 News:** CNN redefined how news is consumed, influencing later networks like **Fox News and MSNBC**.
- **Global Influence:** Its live coverage of wars, political events, and crises solidified its role as a **key player in journalism**.
- **Challenges:** Despite controversies, CNN remains a **major source of breaking news and analysis** in the U.S. and worldwide.
